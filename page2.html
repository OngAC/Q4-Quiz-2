Noli Me Tangere

Jose P. Rizal

For Filipinos, they've probably heard or learned about the book Noli Me Tangere by Jose P. Rizal, their national hero. This book is known for its purpose, written by Rizal as a way of trying to educate the people of their poor situation under the rule of the Spaniards. It was a form of revolt through a book. For this reason, it is now a part of the Philippine education curriculum, providing a glimpse of how life was during those challenging times. The story revolves around a young and wealthy Filipino man who had recently returned to the Philippines after studying abroad and then faced the corrupt and controlling Spanish officials and privileged Filipinos. The main character in this book is named Crisostomo Ibarra, and his background is similar to Rizal's. The story effectively represents the societal issues of the past through different characters and events.

Characters in Noli Me Tangere
Juan Crisostomo Ibarra y Magsalin (Crisostomo Ibarra)
  • A young wealthy man of both Filipino and Spanish decent, the son of Don Rafael Ibarra.
  • Pursued education for 7 years in Europe before coming back to his original country, the Philippines.
  • He was an idealistic man, just like his father, they were righteous and threatened the Spaniards due to their unpleasant treatment towards people, especially the Filipinos against the Spanish church and government.
Maria Clara
  • The daughter of Kapitan Tiago and the childhood sweetheart of Ibarra.
  • Represented the ideal women in the past, being priviliged with qualities such as obedience and innocence.
Padre Damaso Verdolagas (Padre Damaso)
  • An elderly Franciscan priest known for being extremely corrupt, shameless and hateful.
  • With his high authority, he used it to control the lives of the people of who would go against him or simply those he didn’t get along with
Padre Salvi
  • A Spanish priest, younger than Padre Damaso and a lot more cunning than him.
  • Replacing the position of Padre Damaso as friar curate of San Diego, he was a lot more capable as he was a strategist unlike Padre Damaso who is direct with his actions.
Don Rafael Ibarra (Don Rafael)
  • The father of Crisostomo Ibarra who had already been dead during the storyline.
  • He was also an idealistic and righteous man, however it caused him to be killed by Padre Damaso.
  • His death occured during the years that Ibarra was studying in Europe.
Don Anastasio (Pilosopo Tasyo)
  • A man known for being wealthy and intelligent.
  • He was known as a philosopher by the wealthy but known as crazy to the poor.
  • He wasn’t interested in religion in any way and has always preferred education over it.
  • He goes against the government, doing so through many different ways.
Don Santiago de los Santos (Kapitan Tiago)
  • An extremely wealthy Filipino who follows the description of a Filipino who doesn’t support his own people instead goes along with the Spaniards’ racial remarks towards the Filipinos.
  • He extremely values his social status and and his connections.
Basilio
  • The older brother between him and Crispin, being the age of 9.
  • Is a dutiful and caring son and brother although the nigh that Crispin was pulled away, he left him and ran away as a result of fear.
Crispin
  • The younger brother between him and Basilio, being the age of 7.
  • Worked as a sexton along with his brother but was then accused of stealing by their senior sexton which led to the brothers’ suffering.
  • He represents the innocent people who have been falsely accused.
Sisa
  • The mother of Basilio and Crispin.
  • Represents the typical unpriviliged Filipina wives of the past.
  • She regarded her husband like a god and loved him greatly despite the extreme mistreatment she experiences from him.
